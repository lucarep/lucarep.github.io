8_RA: POISSON AND DITTO

2021-11-17 :: Luca

Table of contents

  • INTRO
  • POISSON CAN BE NORMAL
  • POISSON CAN BE UNIFORM
  • POISSON CAN BE EXPONENTIAL
  • CONCLUSIONS

INTRO⌗

If Poisson’s distribution were lumped in with a Pokemon, this would probably be… Ditto. The reason why, for those unfamiliar with Games Freak’s popular franchise is relatively simple. This pokemon is peculiar, because it can take on the appearance of all others. In a certain way this also applies to the distribution of Poisson, let’s see why.

POISSON CAN BE NORMAL⌗

If we notice the histograms of the application shown in post 11_A, we can see that as n increases, the distribution tends to the normal distribution.

POISSON CAN BE UNIFORM⌗

If we take in analysis another histogram of the application, that is the one representing the distribution of the distance of the individual jumps from the origin, what we can observe, is that the distribution in question is referable to a uniform distribution.

POISSON CAN BE EXPONENTIAL⌗

Okay, the last case, let’s look at the histogram representing the distance between two consecutive jumps. What we observe is an exponential trend, then an exponential distribution, however the reason is easily explained. The jumps represent successes, the possibility of having a success in a short period compared to the previous one is relatively higher than having one in a very long period.

CONCLUSIONS⌗

What we have learned from this analysis, is that the dsitribution of Poisson can take on various guises depending on what you observe, do not you find that after all it is a bit similar to Ditto?
